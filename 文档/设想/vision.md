## Goals

Help enforcing freedom of speech. I want to build a BBS(Bulletin board system) without central servers, and stays online as far as there's even one person still having the app.

This app would allow a chance for your voice to be heard by those who want to listen, no matter how it sounds or how little the chance is. No wifi or internet needed, just pure bluetooth.

## Use cases

### wife and husband

It can be a silent messenger between the couple. Even if you got into a cold war, you can leave a post for the other part as clues and hints. The two sides could choose to act that the messages weren't seen and try to fix the relationship simultaneously.

### child and parent

Child at school can write down all he/she thought about during the time at school. When got home, parent can just sync with the child's posts (and the other kids' posts), instead asking all the questions. It saves both sides' time and energy. Similar with parents. Children always want to know what parents' work like.

### coworkers

Office gossips can be done in public, as there's no account info or timestamp. You can even create your own "secret language" to add even more fun.

### neighbors

Instead of knocking on the door to ask for recent news in the neighborhood, the app gives another option to sync. Without going out of the door, you can connect with all the people living in the same building or the blocks of the same section. Same applies with the students in the school.

### strangers passing by each other

99% of the people we pass or get close to on the street or public transportation would never be met again in our lives. Like drivers waiting at traffic lights or stop sign, or passengers in one moving vehicle, train or even plane.

It's a pity to loss the chance to know each other because of the time and space constraint. With the app, it can pickup all the moments in life that the stranger want to share and spead it even further, to become the collective memory of human kind.

### advertisement

Instead of dropping card or packets at everyone's front yard, the broadcast happens whenever you drive by each house. Companies can just offer the latest discounts as a post.

## Difference with BitChat

### Bluetooth only

Focusing on proximity (and saves workload), and avoiding any possible content moderation. So even wifi doesn't need to be supported.

### anonymous

There's no plan to use any encryption, as those who dare to speak out won't mind being found. At the same time, the posts don't show the id or name of the person who writes the post, so that it's impossible to pinpoint the writer just by looking at the posts. Also, there's no room to fake id.

## MVP priorities

### minimum UI

The simpliest UI possible, as the content is the key. It can just have an input field, a button to submit the post, and all the posts from you and other peers. No connection status, only you can tell which posts are not from you because there's no user id displayed for each post, no timestamp, even the app name doesn't need to display. The posts can all be on the right side.

### robust performance

Bluetooth connections can be flaky. Hope the app can perform the best in this domain, and to be the go-to app if anyone likes to test the bluetooth performance of their mobile devices.

### iphone or android

As there are much more android phones, especially in youngsters, it may be better to support Android before iphone.
